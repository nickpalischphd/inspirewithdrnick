Some of the most impactful things you will ever do won't feel important in the moment.

They will feel ordinary. Unremarkable. Even insignificant.

But someone on the other end of your ordinary moment will carry it for years. A word you didn't think twice about. A gesture that cost you nothing. The fact that you showed up when it would have been just as easy to stay home.

This is not about grand gestures. It's about consistency. It's about being present. It's about deciding — again and again — that the people and the moments in front of you are worth your attention.

You don't have to be perfect. You just have to show up.
